Yellowstone National Park - Complete Guide

Overview:
Yellowstone is the first and oldest national park in the world, established on March 1, 1872. Located primarily in Wyoming, with portions in Montana and Idaho, it covers 3,472 square miles (8,991 sq km). Yellowstone sits atop one of the world's largest active volcanic systems, making it home to more geothermal features than any other place on Earth — over 10,000 hydrothermal features including geysers, hot springs, mudpots, and fumaroles.

Location and Access:
Yellowstone has five entrance gates. The North Entrance at Gardiner, Montana is the only entrance open year-round by car. The South Entrance connects to Grand Teton National Park. The East, West, and Northeast Entrances are seasonal. The nearest major airports are in Jackson, Wyoming (60 miles south) and Bozeman, Montana (90 miles north).

Entry Fees:
Private vehicle: $35 per vehicle, valid for 7 days.
Motorcycle: $30, valid for 7 days.
Individual on foot or bicycle: $20, valid for 7 days.
Annual Yellowstone Pass: $70.
America the Beautiful Annual Pass: $80 — covers all national parks for one year, excellent value if visiting multiple parks.

Best Time to Visit:
Summer (June to August) is peak season with all roads and facilities open, warm temperatures (60-80°F), and maximum wildlife activity. Expect large crowds and book lodging 6-12 months in advance. Spring (April to May) offers fewer crowds and newborn wildlife but some roads may still be closed. Fall (September to October) is excellent for wildlife viewing especially elk rut, with cooler temperatures and thinning crowds. Winter (November to March) transforms the park into a snowy wonderland — accessible only by snowcoach or snowmobile, but offers incredible solitude and bison in the snow.

Top Attractions:
1. Old Faithful Geyser: The most famous geyser in the world, erupting every 60-110 minutes to heights of 100-180 feet. The Old Faithful Inn nearby is a National Historic Landmark worth visiting.
2. Grand Prismatic Spring: The largest hot spring in the United States and third largest in the world. Its vivid rainbow colors — blue, green, yellow, orange, and red — come from heat-loving bacteria called thermophiles. Best viewed from the overlook trail.
3. Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone: A dramatic canyon carved by the Yellowstone River, stretching 20 miles long and up to 1,200 feet deep. Artist Point offers the most iconic view of the Lower Falls.
4. Lamar Valley: Known as the "Serengeti of North America," this valley offers the best wildlife watching in the park. Wolf packs, grizzly bears, bison herds, elk, pronghorn, and coyotes are commonly spotted at dawn and dusk.
5. Mammoth Hot Springs: Terraced travertine formations that look like a frozen waterfall. The area also hosts the park headquarters and a historic fort.
6. Norris Geyser Basin: The hottest and most dynamic geyser basin in the park, home to Steamboat Geyser — the world's tallest active geyser, capable of erupting over 300 feet.

Wildlife:
Yellowstone is home to the largest concentration of mammals in the lower 48 states. Animals include: bison (approximately 5,000), grizzly bears (700+), black bears, gray wolves (reintroduced in 1995, now 100+), elk, moose, pronghorn, bighorn sheep, mountain goats, coyotes, foxes, river otters, and bald eagles. Always stay at least 100 yards from bears and wolves, and 25 yards from all other wildlife.

Top Hiking Trails:
1. Grand Prismatic Overlook Trail — 1.6 miles round trip, easy, spectacular aerial view of the spring.
2. Mount Washburn Trail — 6 miles round trip, moderate-strenuous, 360-degree summit views.
3. Mystic Falls Trail — 2.6 miles round trip, easy-moderate, beautiful waterfall.
4. Fairy Falls Trail — 5.4 miles round trip, easy, leads to a stunning 197-foot waterfall.
5. Black Canyon of the Yellowstone — 18 miles one way, strenuous backcountry trail.

Camping:
Yellowstone has 12 campgrounds with over 2,000 sites. Madison, Canyon, Bridge Bay, and Fishing Bridge RV Park require advance reservations (book at recreation.gov). Grant Village, Lewis Lake, and others are first-come, first-served. Backcountry camping requires a permit ($3 per person per night).

Where to Stay:
In-park lodges include Old Faithful Inn, Lake Yellowstone Hotel, Canyon Lodge, and Mammoth Hot Springs Hotel. All are operated by Xanterra and should be booked far in advance. Gateway towns include West Yellowstone (Montana), Gardiner (Montana), and Cody (Wyoming).

Tips for Visitors:
- Arrive at popular spots like Old Faithful before 8am or after 6pm to avoid peak crowds.
- Cell service is extremely limited throughout the park — download offline maps.
- Gas stations are located at Canyon, Fishing Bridge, Grant Village, and Old Faithful.
- Altitude ranges from 5,000 to 11,000 feet — stay hydrated and watch for altitude sickness.
- Never approach geothermal features — the ground can be thin and water temperatures exceed 200°F.
- The boardwalks around thermal features are mandatory — 20 people have died from falling into hot springs.
